# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Boardsmith is for private woodworking and craft planning by a cautious maker who wants help turning a small project idea into a reviewable plan. The primary user is dogfooding real workshop ideas, comparing generated plans against practical constraints, and checking dimensions, materials, fasteners, tool safety, and print readability before any cutting or building.

## Product Purpose

Boardsmith generates detailed woodworking and craft project plans from structured intake, deterministic project templates, and schema-validated AI output. Its job is to make planning clearer and safer for small beginner-friendly projects, not to certify a build or produce fabrication-ready files. Success means a user can create a project, generate or revise a validated plan, review warnings and assumptions, print a browser-based shop sheet, and decide what still needs manual verification.

The interface should help users move from a rough project idea to a builder-ready review packet while preserving the planning-aid boundary. Users should always understand what project they are working on, whether a plan exists, what needs review before building, what the next best action is, and what is safe to print, archive, edit, or regenerate.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, builder-ready, warm, organized, trustworthy, precise, low-noise.

The product should feel modern and user-friendly without becoming glossy or promotional. It should read like a clean digital workbench: direct language, calm hierarchy, visible review steps, and no overconfident claims.

## Anti-references

Boardsmith should not look or sound like a SaaS marketing site, maker-hype landing page, playful AI toy, hobby blog, CAD or professional engineering package, Etsy or marketplace workflow, shopping assistant, or public portfolio system. Avoid language or visuals that imply structural approval, load ratings, child-safety certification, fabrication readiness, export/CAD/CNC output, public sharing, payments, subscriptions, inventory, pricing, vendors, or production multi-user readiness.

## Design Principles

1. Show the next safe action. Each screen should make the current lifecycle state obvious: draft, ready to generate, blocked, ready to review, archived, printable, or read-only.
2. Keep review work visible. Safety flags, assumptions, unresolved dimensions, material checks, cut-list issues, and manual-review reminders should be easy to find before build instructions feel complete.
3. Prefer practical density. The app should support repeated private use with compact, scannable surfaces instead of decorative storytelling.
4. Preserve private-MVP boundaries. Product copy should reinforce that Boardsmith is private and planning-aid-only, without drifting into launch, certification, export, marketplace, or auth promises.
5. Let real dogfood drive polish. Improve workflows when repeated manual use reveals friction; avoid feature growth that does not solve a concrete planning or review problem.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA where practical for private MVP surfaces. Maintain readable contrast, keyboard-friendly form and review flows, clear focus states, descriptive link and button labels, reduced-motion-safe interactions, and layouts that remain understandable at small viewport widths. Safety and review warnings should not rely on color alone.
